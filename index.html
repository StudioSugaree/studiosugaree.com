about

i’m a brand strategist and systems thinker. for two decades, i’ve shaped brands people wear, play, use, eat, and invest with — spanning global icons (adidas, xbox, riot games) and fintech, web3, and wellness startups.

i build brand systems that scale emotionally as well as structurally — identity, story, product experience, and the connective tissue between them. the smallest things say the most. the best brands feel unmistakably human.

experience

over two decades, i’ve worked across the spectrum of brand — from global giants to startups. i’ve built teams and creative systems. repositioned legacy brands. launched new ones from zero. navigated crisis, growth, and reinvention.

on one end: large-scale, culture-shaping work built for global scale. on the other: hands-on execution, emerging technologies, and lean teams pushing boundaries with creativity and code.

where ambition meets complexity

when i’m useful

i work with teams at pivotal moments — when vision starts to outpace structure; when early traction needs something deeper to keep growing; when product alone can’t carry the weight of belief; or when it’s simply time for what’s next.

this isn’t “brand” as decoration. it’s brand as an operating system — the coherence that lets you scale without losing the human part.

what this looks like

  • positioning and brand architecture
  • narrative, voice, and messaging systems
  • creative direction and launch strategy
  • experience coherence across touchpoints (product → support → comms)
  • founder/team alignment: what we’re building, for whom, and why it matters

if you’re building something ambitious with a lean team, we’ll design the system — then make it real.

thoughts on brand, culture, and systems

why brands feel stuck

brands are trapped between broadcast-era thinking and performance-only growth. the opportunity is building coherence across fragmented touchpoints — with soul intact.

everything is brand

the receipt. the apology email. the help article. these “mundane” moments often carry more truth than the headline campaign.
